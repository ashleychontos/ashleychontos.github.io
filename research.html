About Me

Aloha! I am a non-traditional, first-generation graduate student at the Institute for Astronomy (IfA) at the University of Hawai'i at Mānoa. I work on many things stars & planets but I am particularly interested in planet-star interactions and how these might change throughout a system's lifetime. I use data from space-based missions Kepler, K2, and TESS to extract the most precise stellar and planet properties possible to see what we can learn from them. I am currently in my 5th year of graduate school working with Daniel Huber. I am currently funded through the National Science Foundation's Graduate Research Fellowship Program (NSF GRFP), which has allowed me to do part of my PhD at MIT working with Sara Seager. I have been working with her on target selection for a Small Sat Concept Study looking for nearby Earth analogues which was recently selected by NASA for a one year concept study.
