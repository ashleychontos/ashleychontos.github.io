The Boy Scouts of Hawai'i

For years, I've worked closely with folks at the Pearl Harbor Aviation Museum, organizing, staffing and volunteering for astronomy merit badge workshops for The Boy Scouts of Hawai’i, which were typically held twice a year.

The workshops are typically all day and cover everything from the phases of the moon to solar viewing, as well as more hands-on activities like the scale solar system, telescope training and an indoor planetarium show (Starlab).

The last event that we were able to do pre-COVID was particularly special, staffed and led by an all-female astronomer team but also joined by two young female scouts — the first-ever time that females were allowed to join The Boy Scouts of Hawai’i, a momentous step forward and a very memorable event!
