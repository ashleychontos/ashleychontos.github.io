Exoplanet Characterization

KOI 4

When the Kepler spacecraft launched in 2009, the first 3 Kepler Objects of Interest (KOI) were already known from ground-based observations. This makes KOI 4 Kepler's first new exoplanet detection. KOI 4 is a very unique system, thus making it not a fun system to model.

TOI 197

Before the initial Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) data dump, TESS "alerts" were announced to coordinate follow-up observations. This meant releasing light curve data and information for each TESS Object of Interest (TOI). So, what started as the 97th planet candidate (PC) for TESS ended up becoming the very 1st asteroseismic detection for the mission.
