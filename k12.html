Punahou Speaker Series

I connected with a vibrant, enthusiastic high-school science teacher through a different outreach event in 2017, Tiffany Coke, who teaches an upper-level elective astronomy class at Punahou School. Over the first semester and for our trial run, her and I organized times for various astronomy graduate students to visit her class and talk to her students about their research.

Below was our very first trial run with IfA grad, Michael Tucker, teaching the students about various research in transient astronomy.

The high school students raved about the experience, which was equally rewarding (if not more) for the graduate students. Therefore I continued to organize these classes, where we enough volunteers that I could align relevant research topics with the curriculum of the class. Ultimately it became a permanent part of her class and the near-peer mentoring still continues to this day.
